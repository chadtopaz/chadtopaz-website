Privacy Policy

Rebuttinator for Mac

What this policy covers

This policy applies to the Rebuttinator for Mac application sold on the Mac App Store. It does not cover the free web version of Rebuttinator at chadtopaz.com/rebuttinator, the chadtopaz.com website at large, or any other tool published by Chad M. Topaz.

What data does Rebuttinator collect?

None. Rebuttinator is a sandboxed macOS application that does not have permission to access the network. The app cannot collect, transmit, or share any of your work, your activity, or any information about you or your device. There is no analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, advertising, or third-party software development kit (SDK) integrated into the app.

How does Rebuttinator handle the documents I work on?

All source text, annotations, exported HTML, exported PDFs, and saved .rebuttal project files stay on your Mac. They are read from and written to file system locations you choose, using the standard macOS file picker. The app cannot read other files or folders without your explicit choice through that picker.

If you save your work to iCloud Drive, that file is then handled by macOS and Apple's iCloud service under Apple's privacy practices, not ours. Rebuttinator itself plays no role in syncing.

If you share a .rebuttal file or an exported HTML/PDF with someone else, that becomes your action under your control — Rebuttinator has no involvement.

Does Rebuttinator use AI or send my content to any AI service?

No. The app does not use any generative artificial intelligence features. Spell check and grammar suggestions come from the standard macOS text input subsystems and run on your device. Apple Intelligence Writing Tools (Make Friendly, Make Concise, Rewrite, Summarize, etc.) are explicitly disabled in this app's text inputs.

Does Rebuttinator open external links?

The app contains a Help menu item that opens https://www.chadtopaz.com/rebuttinator/ in your default browser when you choose it. That action is initiated by you. The app itself does not establish network connections.

Documents you create may contain links you typed in (for example, a URL added as evidence for an annotation). When you export your rebuttal as HTML or PDF and someone opens it, those links may be clickable in the recipient's browser. That is the recipient's interaction with the open web, not anything Rebuttinator does.

Children

Rebuttinator is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this same URL with a new "Last updated" date. Material changes will also be noted in an app update's release notes.
